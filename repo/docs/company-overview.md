## Business Model

### Revenue Structure (Per Client)

| Component | Amount | Timing |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Setup Fee | Varies | Paid upfront at contract signing |
| Retainer | ~$4,000/month base | Paid after 80% booking target hit |
| Ad Spend % | 10% of monthly ad spend | Added to retainer |
| Scalability fee (10%) | Aligns incentives — higher spend = more leads = more work = higher fee |

**Example:** Client spending $5,000/month on ads → retainer = $4,000 + $500 (10%) = $4,500/month.

### The 28-Day Cycle

The billing model is performance-based, not time-based:

1. Client signs → pays setup fee → onboarding call scheduled
2. Ads launch → **clock starts**
3. Pod manager works the account for up to 28 days
4. At 28 days: if 80%+ of agreed booking target is hit → **bill the retainer**
5. If target not hit: **cycle extends at no charge** until target is reached
6. New cycle starts immediately after billing
7. Goal: **Speed to Cashflow** — 60 days or less from signed contract to first payment. Company average is 75–80 days.

### Why Speed to Cashflow Matters

The agency only gets paid when the cycle closes. Every day an account isn't launched = a day the clock isn't running = delayed payment. Delays compound across a pod of 25 accounts.

### The 80% Rule

- Agreed target (from sales call): e.g., 20 booked appointments per cycle
- Minimum to bill: 80% × 20 = **16 appointments**
- If client is at 10 by Day 28: extend, don't bill, don't accept less than 16

## ICP (Ideal Client Profile)

- **Revenue:** $2.5M+ annually
- **Tenure:** 3+ years in business
- **Mindset:** Growth-oriented owner, not complacent
- **Ad spend capacity:** $3,000+/month
- **Tech comfort:** Can navigate Meta Business Manager or has someone who can
- **Markets:** USA (primarily Sun Belt, Florida, Southeast, Mid-Atlantic, Midwest — varies)

**Why ICP matters for pod managers:** Smaller contractors churn when leads don't immediately convert into signed jobs. Larger contractors understand pipeline, have sales teams, and are more resilient to a slow week.

## Culture & Operating Values

- **Radical ownership:** You own every client relationship in your pod. Their success or failure is on you.
- **No egos:** Everyone helps, questions are welcome, feedback is direct.
- **Feedback-first:** Weekly review calls are high-fidelity feedback loops, not status meetings. Come with action steps, not just reports.
- **Speed:** The bottleneck is always time-to-launch. Make every hour matter.
- **Transparency:** If you're behind, say so. Hiding problems makes them worse.
