# Onboarding Curriculum — Pod Manager Days 1–10

## Pedagogical Philosophy

This is a **performance-based** onboarding, not a knowledge dump. The goal is not that Sam and Patrick can recite facts — it's that they can run a client onboarding call independently by Day 3, post a proper Monday update by Day 4, and operate without hand-holding by Day 10.

Every day has a **learning objective** and a **performance objective**. The quiz tests the learning; the checklist items build toward the performance.

**The 3 things they must internalize above everything else:**
1. Layer 1 before Layer 2. Always.
2. Cost per Booking = spend ÷ appointments. Not leads.
3. Speed to Cashflow is the only metric that feeds the company. Every day an account isn't launched is a day the clock isn't running.

---

## 10-Day Structure

### Day 1 — Foundation Day
**Learning objective:** Understand the business model, the funnel, the operating rhythm, and who does what.  
**Performance objective:** Have system access, know where to find everything, sent intro messages to key team members.  
**Quiz:** Day 1 Knowledge Check (7 questions) — billing model, 80% rule, team structure, Logbook access

**Key content:**
- Company Foundations presentation (75 slides — 2 hour session with Jonathan)
- Recording library: Service Delivery Parts 1 & 2, GHL build, Roofing/HVAC/Gutter onboarding calls
- System access: GHL, Meta, ClickUp, Logbook, Command Center, Account Master Dashboard
- Team introductions: Emmanuel, Leila, Ken

---

### Day 2 — Service Delivery Deep Dive
**Learning objective:** Understand the full account setup flow and A2P (GHL) process.  
**Performance objective:** Do ONE training setup with Emmanuel. Know you'll never do it again.  
**Quiz:** Day 2 Knowledge Check (7 questions) — tools, A2P, setups, Meta placements

**Key content:**
- Do one GHL sub-account setup in training — to understand the process
- A2P = GoHighLevel phone registration, NOT Meta. Emmanuel does all A2P.
- Service Delivery SOP (know where it is, not every step)
- Tool fluency: GHL, Meta Ads Manager, Logbook, Command Center, ClickUp

**Critical callout:** "You do ONE setup in training to understand the process. After that, Emmanuel and Mervin handle all setups. Task them via ClickUp."

---

### Day 3 — First Account Deep Dive
**Learning objective:** Apply the Layer 1/Layer 2 diagnostic framework to real accounts.  
**Performance objective:** Identify and document the health status and action steps for every active account.  
**Quiz:** Day 3 Knowledge Check (6 questions) — Layer 1/2, CAPI, survey conversion

**Key content:**
- Layer 1 first: bookings vs target, cost per booking, health color
- Layer 2 only when Layer 1 fails: CPC, CTR, survey conversion, OSA rate
- CAPI verification: all accounts should have pixel conditioning active
- First client touchpoints: introduce yourself, pull closer call recordings

---

### Day 4 — Operating Rhythm
**Learning objective:** Master the weekly update and review call rhythm.  
**Performance objective:** Post a complete, detailed Monday/Thursday status update.  
**Quiz:** Days 4–5 Knowledge Check (5 questions)

**Key content:**
- Status update format: per-account breakdown with health color, days elapsed, bookings vs target, action steps with dates
- Review call preparation: know your accounts before the call, come with action steps
- Cycle management: know billing windows, initiate renewals 5–7 days before cycle end
- Billing failures: call the client same day — no exceptions

---

### Day 5 — Creative & Advertising Deep Dive
**Learning objective:** Know the Meta ad standards and creative requirements well enough to QA a creative set.  
**Performance objective:** Review all active ad sets against the 6 placement rule and creative standard.  
**Quiz:** Continued from Day 4–5 quiz

**Key content:**
- 6 approved placements (memorize these)
- Creative Mastery standard: 5 Singles + 5 Two-Folds + 5 Tri-Folds = 15 ads per set
- Photo types: Trust, Service, Brand
- Ad Set Reviewer: https://ad-set-reviewer.vercel.app/
- Advantage+ must be OFF (Meta re-enables it on duplication)
- B2C keyword in all campaign names

---

### Day 6 — VA Management & Logbook
**Learning objective:** Understand how to work with the VA team and read the Logbook.  
**Performance objective:** Review all open leads in the Logbook and ensure VAs are working them.  
**Quiz:** Week 2 Knowledge Check (6 questions)

**Key content:**
- VAs call every lead within 4 hours, qualify, book, update Logbook
- Qualified tag → CAPI fires → Meta pixel gets data
- Leila = Head of VA Management. Escalate quality issues to her, not to individual VAs.
- Live transfer process: transfer only counts if the lead books. If no booking, VA follows up again.
- Open leads (white) = revenue waiting to happen. Check Logbook daily.

---

### Days 7–8 — First Review Call + Revenue Activities
**Learning objective:** Execute a review call and understand the revenue multiplication playbook.  
**Performance objective:** Present all accounts on a live review call. Identify 2+ upsell/referral opportunities.

**Key content:**
- Review call format: Layer 1 per account, action steps, questions
- Revenue activities: testimonials, referrals, upsells (ad spend), cross-sells (new markets), cycle renewals
- Communication standards: 24–48 hour rule, billing failure = phone call, "leads suck" = ask for sales data
- When to escalate to Oscar: client retention threats, pricing disputes

---

### Days 9–10 — Creative Refresh Protocol + Wrap
**Learning objective:** Know the full creative refresh decision tree and pixel management.  
**Performance objective:** Pass the 15-question Final Comprehensive Quiz with 80%+.

**Key content:**
- Context check before any creative decision (holidays, learning phase, payment failures)
- Post-Andromeda protocol: duplicate ad set → if fails → full refresh
- CAPI / pixel conditioning verification across all accounts
- Campaign naming (B2C keyword)
- 10-day self-assessment checklist
